CYBERBULLYING What is it?

Cyberbullying is bullying with the use of digital technologies.
It can take place on social media, messaging platforms,
gaming platforms and mobile phones.

It is repeated behaviour, aimed at scaring,
angering or shaming those who are targeted.

Let's take a closer look at
these escalating cases of cyberbullying.

In 2010, Tyler Clementi,
a freshman at Rutgers University in New Jersey,
committed suicide after his roommate
secretly streamed his same-sex encounter
and mocked him online.

In 2012, 15-year-old Amanda Todd took her
own life after enduring online stalking and
cyberbullying.
She posted a video on YouTube
sharing her experiences, but the harassment
continued afterward.

In 2013, 12-year-old Rebecca Sedwick from
Florida took her own life after enduring
prolonged cyberbullying from schoolmates.
One of the perpetrators posted a message
saying, "I can brag that I killed her."

In 2016, 18-year-old Brandi Belle from Texas
experienced severe stress due to persistent
cyberbullying, body shaming, and fake online
dating profiles. The perpetrators leaked her
personal information and sent insulting
messages. Eventually, she took her own life in
front of her family.
